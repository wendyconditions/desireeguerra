Desiree Guerra

Bio

Desiree has always been captivated by the arts in many ways. Growing up, she frequently would be found working on creative projects such as drawing, painting, building, filming, playing music, exploring colors, you name it. If it was created and brought to life, she would be the first to question it and to dissect how it came into fruition.

At the age of 18, a month out of high school, Desiree decided to pursue a career in Graphic Design. She enrolled at the Art Institute of California - Orange County, with the intention of learning and becoming relevant in today’s culture emphasizing in technology and design. Dissecting the design world and discerning how much our culture is being greatly impacted by it captivated her so immensely, she knew this was the adventure she wanted to study, research, and discover skills in.

Once Desiree graduated and earned her Bachelor's of Arts degree, she went on to be accepted into the undergraduate program at Disney Consumer Products in Los Angeles. She was there for almost a year learning, experiencing and being equipped by the best in the business. It was 2009, the year the economy collapsed so as things shifted, she found herself traveling into the ad agency world of Orange County where she currently resides. She has been in the design industry for 9 years now having worked with fortune 500 companies such as Disney, Toms Shoes, Motorola, Carnival Corporation & Plc to local businesses such as Old Town Roasting, The Muddy Hive and Nobleman Magazine in her community. Working in the corporate industry has broaden Desiree's palette to, not only succeed in creative problem solving, but also, in adapting and creating new and different environments.

“Before diving into the fine art world, I wanted to take time and learn everything I could about the creative technology world. I want to be able to incorporate new techniques and reinvent the way fine art is looked at.”

Taking what she has learned and experienced in the design industry into her fine art has brought her full circle back to her true passion. “Creating fine art that tells a story and carries a consistent message has been a game changer. I don’t want my work to just be ‘another painting’ on a wall or something to glance at while passing by. I want it to evoke an emotion, a memory, recall a story, and inspire others to feel comfortable to express themselves.”

Having a professional background has taught Desiree to look at everything as a whole; from the art pieces, to the branding, to the marketing and business side of it all. “Every aspect of an art series isn’t only about the pieces. It's matched with just as much physical and mental work, in order, to have it be presentable to the public in branding, marketing and communication.”

Desiree’s approach is well rounded, accomplished, and conducive, in being able to showcase a new and fresh way to experience fine art.
